# PlantVillage Disease Reference Guide
## Comprehensive Disease Identification & Management for AP Farmers

> This guide covers all 38 PlantVillage disease classes with symptoms, causes, and management strategies. Special focus on crops grown in Andhra Pradesh.

---

## APPLE DISEASES

### 1. Apple Scab (Venturia inaequalis)
- **Crop:** Apple
- **Symptoms:** Olive-green to brown velvety spots on leaves and fruit. Leaves may curl and drop early. Fruit shows dark, scabby lesions reducing market value.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Venturia inaequalis)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Cool, wet spring weather (10-24 degC), prolonged leaf wetness
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Captan 2g/L or Mancozeb 2.5g/L at pink bud and petal fall stages
  - Organic: Sulfur dust 3kg/ha, remove and destroy infected leaves, avoid overhead irrigation

---

### 2. Apple Black Rot (Botryosphaeria obtusa)
- **Crop:** Apple
- **Symptoms:** Brown circular lesions on fruit with concentric rings (frog-eye pattern). Cankers on branches with reddish-brown discoloration. Leaves show purple-bordered spots.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Botryosphaeria obtusa)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Warm, humid weather; wounds from hail or insects; dead wood
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Thiophanate-methyl 1g/L or Captan 2g/L
  - Organic: Prune and destroy infected branches, apply copper-based sprays, improve orchard sanitation

---

### 3. Apple Cedar Rust (Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae)
- **Crop:** Apple (alternates on cedar/juniper)
- **Symptoms:** Bright orange-yellow spots on upper leaf surface with tube-like structures on underside. Fruit lesions near calyx end.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Moist spring conditions, presence of nearby cedar/juniper hosts
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Myclobutanil 1ml/L or Propiconazole 1ml/L at pink bud stage
  - Organic: Remove alternate hosts (cedar/juniper) within 1km, plant resistant varieties

---

## BLUEBERRY DISEASES

### 4. Blueberry Powdery Mildew (Erysiphe vaccinii)
- **Crop:** Blueberry
- **Symptoms:** White powdery coating on young leaves, shoots, and fruit. Infected leaves curl and become distorted. Fruit may crack or fail to ripen.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Erysiphe vaccinii)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Warm days, cool nights, low humidity, dense canopy
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Wettable sulfur 3g/L or Triadimefon 1ml/L
  - Organic: Potassium bicarbonate 5g/L spray, improve air circulation by pruning, neem oil 3ml/L

---

## CHERRY DISEASES

### 5. Cherry Powdery Mildew (Podosphaera clandestina)
- **Crop:** Cherry (Sweet and Sour)
- **Symptoms:** White powdery patches on young leaves, shoots, and fruit. New growth is stunted and distorted. Infected fruit develops russeting.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Podosphaera clandestina)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Moderate temperatures (15-25 degC), shaded dense canopy, dry weather with high humidity at night
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Myclobutanil 1ml/L or Sulfur 3g/L
  - Organic: Neem oil 3ml/L, baking soda spray (5g/L), prune for air circulation

---

## CORN / MAIZE DISEASES

### 6. Corn Gray Leaf Spot (Cercospora zeae-maydis)
- **Crop:** Maize/Corn
- **Symptoms:** Rectangular, gray to tan lesions with parallel sides running between leaf veins. Lesions may coalesce causing large blighted areas. Severe infection kills leaves.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Cercospora zeae-maydis)
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity, temperatures 25-30 degC, prolonged dew periods, reduced tillage fields
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Propiconazole 1ml/L or Azoxystrobin 1ml/L at VT stage
  - Organic: Resistant hybrids, crop rotation with non-host crops (soybean), deep plowing

### 7. Corn Common Rust (Puccinia sorghi)
- **Crop:** Maize/Corn
- **Symptoms:** Small, oval to elongated brick-red pustules on both leaf surfaces. Pustules turn dark brown-black as crop matures. Heavy infection weakens plants.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Puccinia sorghi)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Cool temperatures (15-25 degC), high humidity, dew
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Mancozeb 2.5g/L or Tebuconazole 1ml/L
  - Organic: Plant resistant hybrids, early planting to avoid peak rust season

### 8. Corn Northern Leaf Blight (Exserohilum turcicum)
- **Crop:** Maize/Corn
- **Symptoms:** Long, elliptical, gray-green to tan lesions (cigar-shaped), 2.5-15cm long. Lesions appear first on lower leaves and move upward. Severely blighted fields appear frosted.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Exserohilum turcicum)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Moderate temperatures (18-27 degC), high humidity, dew, cloudy weather
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Mancozeb 2.5g/L or Propiconazole 1ml/L
  - Organic: Resistant varieties, crop rotation, deep plowing to bury crop debris

## GRAPE DISEASES

### 9. Grape Black Rot (Guignardia bidwellii)
- **Crop:** Grape
- **Symptoms:** Reddish-brown circular spots on leaves with dark border. Fruit turns brown, shrivels, and becomes mummified (hard black raisins hanging on vine).
- **Cause:** Fungal (Guignardia bidwellii)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Warm, wet weather (21-26 degC), rain during bloom, high humidity
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Myclobutanil 1ml/L or Mancozeb 2.5g/L from bud break
  - Organic: Remove mummified fruit, improve canopy management, copper-based sprays

### 10. Grape Esca / Black Measles (Phaeomoniella chlamydospora + others)
- **Crop:** Grape
- **Symptoms:** Interveinal leaf chlorosis with brown/red discoloration (tiger stripe pattern). Fruit shows small dark spots (measles). Severe form causes sudden vine collapse (apoplexy).
- **Cause:** Fungal complex (multiple wood-rotting fungi)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Old vines, pruning wounds, hot dry summers following wet winters
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: No curative treatment; protect pruning wounds with fungicide paste (Thiophanate-methyl)
  - Organic: Use clean pruning tools, seal wounds, remove severely infected vines

### 11. Grape Leaf Blight (Pseudocercospora vitis)
- **Crop:** Grape
- **Symptoms:** Angular dark brown spots on leaves bounded by veins. Spots have yellow halos. Severe infection causes premature leaf drop weakening vines.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Pseudocercospora vitis)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Hot, humid weather, poor air circulation in dense canopy
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Copper oxychloride 3g/L or Mancozeb 2.5g/L
  - Organic: Improve canopy training and air flow, remove infected leaves

## ORANGE / CITRUS DISEASES

### 12. Citrus Greening / Huanglongbing (HLB)
- **Crop:** Orange, Lime, Lemon, Sweet Lime (AP important crop)
- **Symptoms:** Blotchy mottling of leaves (asymmetric yellowing, NOT uniform). Fruit remains small, green, lopsided, bitter. Trees decline slowly over years. Root system deteriorates first.
- **Cause:** Bacterial (Candidatus Liberibacter asiaticus) transmitted by psyllid (Diaphorina citri)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Presence of Asian citrus psyllid, warm climate, movement of infected planting material
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Control psyllid vector with Imidacloprid 0.3ml/L or Thiamethoxam 0.2g/L; no cure for infected trees
  - Organic: Remove and destroy infected trees, use certified disease-free nursery plants, sticky yellow traps for psyllid monitoring
  - AP ALERT: HLB is a major threat to AP citrus growers. Report suspected cases to local horticulture department immediately.

## PEACH DISEASES

### 13. Peach Bacterial Spot (Xanthomonas arboricola pv. pruni)
- **Crop:** Peach, Plum, Nectarine
- **Symptoms:** Small, water-soaked spots on leaves turning purple-brown with yellow halos. Spots fall out leaving shot-hole appearance. Fruit shows sunken dark spots with cracked centers.
- **Cause:** Bacterial (Xanthomonas arboricola pv. pruni)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Warm, wet weather (24-28 degC), rain and wind spread, young actively growing tissue
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Copper oxychloride 3g/L sprays at green tip stage through harvest
  - Organic: Resistant varieties, windbreaks to reduce spread, avoid overhead irrigation

---

## BELL PEPPER / CHILLI DISEASES

### 14. Bell Pepper Bacterial Spot (Xanthomonas euvesicatoria)
- **Crop:** Bell Pepper, Chilli (AP major crop)
- **Symptoms:** Small, water-soaked spots on leaves, fruit, and stems. Lesions turn brown with yellow halo. Fruit spots become raised, then sunken with scab-like surface. Defoliation in severe cases.
- **Cause:** Bacterial (Xanthomonas euvesicatoria)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Warm (24-30 degC), high humidity, rain splash, overhead irrigation
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Copper oxychloride 3g/L + Mancozeb 2g/L; Streptomycin sulfate 200ppm for severe cases
  - Organic: Use certified disease-free seed, drip irrigation, avoid working in wet fields, crop rotation
  - AP CONTEXT: Very common in chilli-growing areas of Guntur, Khammam. Use treated seeds and copper sprays from early stage.

## POTATO DISEASES

### 15. Potato Early Blight (Alternaria solani)
- **Crop:** Potato (also Tomato)
- **Symptoms:** Dark brown to black circular spots with concentric rings (target-board pattern) on older leaves first. Yellow halo around spots. Severe infection causes complete leaf death.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Alternaria solani)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Warm temperatures (24-30 degC), alternating wet and dry periods, stressed/nutrient-deficient plants
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Mancozeb 2.5g/L or Chlorothalonil 2g/L or Azoxystrobin 1ml/L every 7-10 days
  - Organic: Balanced NPK nutrition, remove infected lower leaves, copper-based sprays, 3-year crop rotation

### 16. Potato Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans)
- **Crop:** Potato, Tomato (AP most destructive disease)
- **Symptoms:** Water-soaked pale green lesions on leaf edges/tips that turn dark brown rapidly. White sporulating growth on underside of leaves in humid conditions. Entire field can be destroyed within days. Infected tubers show brown rot.
- **Cause:** Oomycete (Phytophthora infestans)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Cool (10-20 degC), wet, cloudy weather; relative humidity above 90%; nights below 12 degC with wet days
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Metalaxyl + Mancozeb (Ridomil Gold) 2.5g/L; Cymoxanil + Mancozeb 3g/L; spray preventively every 7 days
  - Organic: Certified disease-free seed tubers, field monitoring, remove volunteer plants, copper hydroxide 3g/L
  - AP ALERT: Late blight epidemics occur in AP during November-January. Watch for forecasts and spray preventively.

## RASPBERRY

### 17. Raspberry - Healthy
- **Characteristics:** Healthy raspberry plants show bright green compound leaves, vigorous upright canes, and abundant fruit production. No spots, discoloration, or lesions. Good plant health = proper spacing, watering, and balanced nutrition.

## SOYBEAN DISEASES

### 18. Soybean Frogeye Leaf Spot (Cercospora sojina)
- **Crop:** Soybean
- **Symptoms:** Circular spots with gray centers and reddish-purple borders on leaves. Multiple spots merge causing irregular blighted areas. Seed pods can also be infected.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Cercospora sojina)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Warm temperatures (25-30 degC), humid, wet conditions during reproductive stages
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Thiophanate-methyl 1g/L or Azoxystrobin 1ml/L
  - Organic: Resistant varieties, crop rotation, avoid overhead irrigation, balanced K nutrition reduces severity

---

## SQUASH / CUCURBIT DISEASES

### 19. Squash Powdery Mildew (Podosphaera xanthii)
- **Crop:** Squash, Pumpkin, Cucumber, Bitter Gourd (AP cucurbits)
- **Symptoms:** White powdery patches on upper and lower leaf surfaces. Infected leaves turn yellow, brown, and dry. Severely affected plants have reduced yield and fruit quality.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Podosphaera xanthii / Erysiphe cichoracearum)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Warm, dry weather with moderate humidity; dense canopy; late-season crop stress
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Wettable sulfur 3g/L or Triadimefon 1ml/L or Dinocap 1ml/L
  - Organic: Neem oil 3ml/L, potassium bicarbonate spray, remove heavily infected leaves, resistant varieties

---

## STRAWBERRY DISEASES

### 20. Strawberry Leaf Scorch (Diplocarpon earlianum)
- **Crop:** Strawberry
- **Symptoms:** Small, irregular dark purple spots on leaf surfaces. Centers of older spots turn grayish-tan. Severe infection causes leaves to look scorched. Runners and petioles also affected.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Diplocarpon earlianum)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Wet, humid conditions; overhead irrigation; warm temperatures (18-24 degC)
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Myclobutanil 1ml/L or Captan 2g/L
  - Organic: Drip irrigation, remove old infected leaves after harvest, improve row spacing for airflow

## TOMATO DISEASES (AP Priority - Most Important Crop)

Tomato is a major cash crop in AP (Kurnool, Chittoor, Krishna districts). Disease management is critical.

### 21. Tomato Bacterial Spot (Xanthomonas vesicatoria)
- **Crop:** Tomato (also Pepper)
- **Symptoms:** Small, water-soaked spots on leaves that turn dark brown with yellow margins. Fruit shows raised, rough, scab-like spots. Severe infection causes defoliation and unmarketable fruit.
- **Cause:** Bacterial (Xanthomonas vesicatoria)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Warm (24-30 degC), wet, rainy weather; overhead irrigation; high humidity
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Copper oxychloride 3g/L + Streptomycin 200ppm; start at first sign
  - Organic: Use certified seeds, avoid overhead irrigation, copper hydroxide sprays, crop rotation

### 22. Tomato Early Blight (Alternaria solani)
- **Crop:** Tomato, Potato
- **Symptoms:** Dark brown circular spots with distinct concentric rings (bullseye/target pattern), usually on older lower leaves first. Yellow halo surrounds each spot. Severe infection = complete defoliation from bottom up.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Alternaria solani)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Warm (24-30 degC), alternating wet-dry cycles, nutrient-stressed plants, dense planting
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Mancozeb 2.5g/L or Iprodione 2g/L or Azoxystrobin 1ml/L; spray every 10 days
  - Organic: Balanced fertilization, mulching, neem oil 3ml/L, remove infected leaves, 2-year rotation

### 23. Tomato Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans)
- **Crop:** Tomato, Potato
- **Symptoms:** Large, irregular, water-soaked pale green patches on leaves that rapidly turn dark brown/black. White cottony sporulation on underside in humid conditions. Brown lesions on stems. Fruit rots rapidly becoming brown and foul-smelling.
- **Cause:** Oomycete (Phytophthora infestans)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Cool nights (below 12 degC), cloudy days, prolonged leaf wetness, RH above 90%
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Metalaxyl + Mancozeb 2.5g/L (Ridomil Gold); Cymoxanil 0.5g/L; spray preventively every 7 days
  - Organic: Copper hydroxide 3g/L, avoid overhead irrigation, stake plants for better airflow, destroy infected crop immediately

### 24. Tomato Leaf Mold (Passalora fulva)
- **Crop:** Tomato (especially greenhouse/polyhouse)
- **Symptoms:** Pale yellowish-green spots on upper leaf surface. Corresponding olive-green to gray velvety mold growth on lower leaf surface. Infected leaves curl upward and wither.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Passalora fulva / Cladosporium fulvum)
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity (above 85%), poor ventilation, temperatures 20-25 degC; especially in protected cultivation
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Chlorothalonil 2g/L or Mancozeb 2.5g/L; reduce humidity
  - Organic: Improve ventilation in polyhouse, avoid wetting foliage, remove lower infected leaves

### 25. Tomato Septoria Leaf Spot (Septoria lycopersici)
- **Crop:** Tomato
- **Symptoms:** Many small circular spots with dark brown borders and grayish-white centers, each with tiny black specks (pycnidia) in center. Appears first on lower leaves. Heavy infection causes severe defoliation.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Septoria lycopersici)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Wet, humid weather; temperatures 20-25 degC; extended leaf wetness; infected crop debris
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Mancozeb 2.5g/L or Chlorothalonil 2g/L or Thiophanate-methyl 1g/L
  - Organic: Deep plowing, crop rotation (2+ years), remove lower leaves, avoid overhead irrigation, mulching

### 26. Tomato Spider Mites (Tetranychus urticae)
- **Crop:** Tomato, Chilli, Brinjal (AP: common in summer)
- **Symptoms:** Fine stippling/silvery bronzing of leaves. Fine webbing on leaf undersurface. Leaves turn yellow, then brown and die. Severe infestation causes complete crop loss in summer.
- **Cause:** Mite pest (Tetranychus urticae - Two-spotted spider mite)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Hot, dry weather (above 30 degC), drought stress, dust-covered crops, broad-spectrum pesticide use killing natural enemies
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Dicofol 2.5ml/L or Abamectin 0.5ml/L or Spiromesifen 1ml/L; avoid pyrethroids (worsen mites)
  - Organic: Neem oil 3ml/L + wetting agent, water sprays on leaf undersides, release predatory mites, avoid dusty conditions

### 27. Tomato Target Spot (Corynespora cassiicola)
- **Crop:** Tomato
- **Symptoms:** Brown circular spots with concentric rings (target-like). Unlike early blight, spots appear throughout the canopy not just lower leaves. Fruit lesions are dark brown and sunken.
- **Cause:** Fungal (Corynespora cassiicola)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Warm, humid weather, extended leaf wetness, dense planting
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Azoxystrobin 1ml/L or Tebuconazole 1ml/L
  - Organic: Good plant spacing, drip irrigation, remove lower infected leaves

### 28. Tomato Mosaic Virus (ToMV)
- **Crop:** Tomato (also Pepper, Tobacco)
- **Symptoms:** Mosaic pattern of light and dark green on leaves, leaf distortion, curling, and blistering. Stunted plant growth. Fruit may show internal browning (blotchy ripening). No external fruit spots.
- **Cause:** Viral (Tomato Mosaic Virus - ToMV, Tobamovirus)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Mechanical transmission (tools, hands, tobacco use); contact during transplanting, staking, pruning; seed-borne
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: No cure; prevent with insecticide sprays; disinfect tools with 1% bleach or 3% Dettol
  - Organic: Use certified virus-tested seeds, wash hands after tobacco use, do not smoke near crop, rogue and destroy infected plants early, plant resistant varieties

### 29. Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (TYLCV)
- **Crop:** Tomato (AP major disease - highest economic impact)
- **Symptoms:** Upward curling and cupping of young leaves. Leaves turn yellow (chlorotic) and reduced in size. Plants are severely stunted. Flower drop and very poor fruit set. Infected plants look like miniature bushes.
- **Cause:** Viral (Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus - TYLCV, Begomovirus) transmitted by whitefly (Bemisia tabaci)
- **Favorable Conditions:** High whitefly populations, warm dry weather, susceptible varieties, nearby infected plants or alternate hosts (weeds)
- **Management:**
  - Chemical: Control whitefly vector: Imidacloprid 0.3ml/L or Thiamethoxam 0.25g/L at seedling stage; Buprofezin 1.5ml/L
  - Organic: Yellow sticky traps (40/acre) to monitor and trap whiteflies, reflective silver mulch repels whiteflies, neem oil 3ml/L, remove and burn infected plants, plant TYLCV-resistant varieties (TY-1, Arka Rakshak)
  - AP ALERT: TYLCV is devastating in AP tomato crops. Always use resistant varieties. Spray nursery seedlings with Imidacloprid before transplanting.

## HEALTHY PLANT CLASSES

### 30. Apple - Healthy
Bright green, glossy leaves with no spots or discoloration. Firm fruit with natural skin color. Vigorous shoot growth and good branch structure.

### 31. Blueberry - Healthy
Dark green oval leaves, firm blue-purple berries with natural bloom. Healthy canes with no lesions or cankers.

### 32. Cherry - Healthy
Glossy dark green leaves with serrated margins, no spots or halos. Plump, uniformly colored cherries.

### 33. Corn - Healthy
Tall, upright plants with deep green leaves. No rust pustules, lesions, or gray patches. Uniform ear development.

### 34. Grape - Healthy
Bright green palmate leaves with no mottling or spots. Well-developed clusters of uniform-colored berries. No wood lesions.

### 35. Peach - Healthy
Lanceolate glossy leaves, no shot-holes or spots. Firm fruit with natural peach/yellow color, no sunken lesions.

### 36. Bell Pepper - Healthy
Dark glossy green leaves, firm-walled fruit without spots. Upright plants with good branching.

### 37. Potato - Healthy
Dark green leaflets with no spots or blight lesions. Firm stems. Underground tubers are firm with no rot or discoloration.

### 38. Raspberry - Healthy
Bright green compound leaves, no purple spots or mildew. Vigorous upright canes with no cankers.
