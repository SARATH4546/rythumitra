# Andhra Pradesh — Soil Health & Fertilizer Guide
# Source: Soil Health Card Scheme, ICAR, AP Agriculture Department

## Soil Types in Andhra Pradesh

| Region | Soil Type | Crops Suited |
|---|---|---|
| Krishna-Godavari Delta | Alluvial (Entisols) | Paddy, sugarcane, banana |
| Coastal Andhra | Red sandy loam | Tobacco, groundnut, chilli |
| Rayalaseema | Red laterite | Cotton, groundnut, sunflower |
| North AP (Vizag) | Red loamy + hill soils | Coffee, paddy, turmeric |
| North AP plains | Black cotton (Vertisols) | Cotton, maize, sorghum |

## Soil Testing

### Why Test?
- Know exact nutrient status (N, P, K, pH, micronutrients)
- Avoid over-fertilization (saves money, reduces groundwater pollution)
- Identify deficiencies early (pH, Zn, Fe, B, S)

### How to Collect Soil Sample
1. Collect 10–15 soil samples from different spots in field (zigzag pattern)
2. Remove grass/debris from surface
3. Dig 15cm deep, take soil with clean tool
4. Mix all samples in clean bucket
5. Take 500g representative sample
6. Label: your name, village, crop to be grown, date

### Where to Get Tested
- **Soil Health Card (SHC) scheme**: Free soil testing at district Agriculture offices and KVKs
- **KVK labs**: Every district has a Krishi Vigyan Kendra
- **Private labs**: Available in major towns (~Rs 200–500 per sample)
- **Online**: soilhealth.dac.gov.in (find nearest testing center)
- **Helpline**: 1800-180-1551

### Soil pH Guide
| pH | Condition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| <5.5 | Very acidic | Apply lime 2–4 tonnes/ha |
| 5.5–6.5 | Slightly acidic | Most crops do well |
| 6.5–7.5 | Neutral | Ideal for most AP crops |
| 7.5–8.5 | Alkaline | Gypsum + acidifying fertilizers |
| >8.5 | Very alkaline | Gypsum + sulfur + specialized management |

## Fertilizer Recommendations by Crop

### Paddy
| Nutrient | Dose (kg/ha) | Application |
|---|---|---|
| Nitrogen (N) | 120 | Split: 1/3 basal + 1/3 at tillering + 1/3 panicle initiation |
| Phosphorus (P) | 60 | Full dose at basal |
| Potassium (K) | 60 | Half basal + half tillering |
| Zinc sulfate | 25 | If deficient (pale yellow strips on young leaves) |

### Cotton
| Nutrient | Dose (kg/ha) |
|---|---|
| N | 180 (4 splits) |
| P | 90 (basal) |
| K | 90 (half basal + half at squaring) |
| Sulfur | 40 (basal, improves fiber quality) |
| Boron | 1 (foliar spray at bud initiation) |

### Tomato
| Nutrient | Dose (kg/ha) |
|---|---|
| N | 180 (6 splits via drip or manual) |
| P | 120 (basal) |
| K | 100 (basal + 2 splits) |
| Calcium | 100 (prevents blossom end rot) |
| Boron | 1 spray (fruit set) |

### Chilli
N: 120 | P: 60 | K: 40 kg/ha
Magnesium sulfate 10kg/ha foliar spray reduces leaf curl symptoms.

### Groundnut
N: 25 | P: 50 | K: 0 kg/ha + **Gypsum 500kg/ha at pegging** (essential for pod filling)
Boron 1kg/ha foliar spray at pegging.

### Maize
N: 180 | P: 80 | K: 60 kg/ha
Zinc sulfate 25kg/ha if deficient.

### Sugarcane
N: 300 | P: 120 | K: 120 kg/ha over 12 months in 4 splits.

## Micronutrient Deficiency Guide

| Symptom | Likely Deficiency | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Young leaves yellow (overall) | Nitrogen (N) | Top-dress Urea 30kg/ha |
| Purple/reddish leaves | Phosphorus (P) | Apply DAP or SSP |
| Leaf edges brown/scorched | Potassium (K) | Apply MOP (Muriate of Potash) |
| Interveinal chlorosis (yellow between green veins) — young leaves | Iron (Fe) | Ferrous sulfate 0.5% spray (5g/L) |
| Interveinal chlorosis — older leaves | Manganese (Mn) | Manganese sulfate 0.5% spray |
| Pale yellow strips on young paddy leaves | Zinc (Zn) | Zinc sulfate 0.5% spray or 25kg/ha soil |
| Small, distorted terminal leaves | Boron (B) | Borax 10kg/ha or boric acid 0.2% spray |
| Yellowing with green veins (overall) | Sulfur (S) | Gypsum 100kg/ha or elemental sulfur |
| Yellowing from tips — banana, papaya | Potassium or Calcium | K+ or lime application |

## Organic Fertilizer Options

| Source | N-P-K Content | Application Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Farm Yard Manure (FYM) | 0.5-0.25-0.5% | 10 tonnes/ha | Apply 3 weeks before sowing |
| Vermicompost | 1.5-1.0-0.5% | 2–4 tonnes/ha | Premium quality, fast acting |
| Green manure (Dhaincha/Sesbania) | — | Plow in at 45 days | Adds ~80 kg N equivalent/ha |
| Poultry manure | 3-2.5-2% | 3–5 tonnes/ha | Rich in N, P; apply well before sowing |
| Neem cake | 4-1-1.5% | 150–200kg/ha | Also acts as nematicide |

### Biofertilizers (Free Living / Symbiotic Nitrogen Fixers)
| Biofertilizer | Crop | Dose | Benefit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rhizobium | Groundnut, pulses | 200g/kg seed | Fixes 50–100 kg N/ha |
| Azospirillum | Paddy, maize, wheat | 3–4 packets/ha | Fixes 20–30 kg N/ha |
| Phosphate Solubilizing Bacteria (PSB) | All crops | 3–4 packets/ha | Makes fixed P available |

## Soil Health Card (SHC) — How to Use
1. Get tested (see above)
2. Card shows: pH, N, P, K, S, Zn, Fe, Mn, Cu, B, OC status
3. Follow the crop-wise fertilizer dose printed on your card
4. Cards valid for 3 years; re-test after that
5. Portal: soilhealth.dac.gov.in
6. Helpline: 1800-180-1551 (free, Mon–Sat 6am–10pm)
