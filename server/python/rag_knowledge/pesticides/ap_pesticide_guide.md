# AP Pesticide Reference Guide
## Safe and Effective Pesticide Use for Andhra Pradesh Farmers

> IMPORTANT: Always read label instructions before use. Wear protective equipment. Follow waiting periods before harvest. Keep children and animals away from sprayed fields.

---

## SECTION 1: INSECTICIDES

### 1.1 Brown Plant Hopper (BPH) in Paddy

BPH is the number one paddy pest in AP delta regions. It causes "hopper burn" - circular patches of dried, brown paddy plants.

| Chemical | Trade Name | Dose | Waiting Period |
|----------|-----------|------|----------------|
| Thiamethoxam 25WG | Actara | 0.2g/L (100g/acre) | 14 days |
| Buprofezin 25SC | Applaud | 1.5ml/L | 14 days |
| Pymetrozine 50WG | Chess | 0.3g/L | 14 days |
| Imidacloprid 17.8SL | Confidor | 0.3ml/L | 21 days |

**How to apply:** Direct spray at the base of plant (panicle region). Do NOT spray during flowering (kills pollinators). Apply early morning or evening.

**Signs of BPH:** Plants turn brown in circular patches. Lift plants - see tiny white/brown insects at base. Honeydew deposit gives sticky feeling.

**Organic alternative:** Drain water from field for 3-4 days (desiccation method). This kills hoppers at base. Do NOT use during critical water stages.

### 1.2 Stem Borer in Paddy

Dead heart in vegetative stage; white ear (chalky panicle) in reproductive stage are key symptoms.

| Chemical | Trade Name | Dose | Waiting Period |
|----------|-----------|------|----------------|
| Chlorpyrifos 20EC | Durban | 2.5ml/L | 21 days |
| Cartap hydrochloride 50SP | Padan | 1g/L | 14 days |
| Fipronil 5SC | Regent | 1.5ml/L | 30 days |
| Chlorantraniliprole 18.5SC | Coragen | 0.3ml/L | 14 days |

**AP timing:** Apply at egg hatching stage. Use pheromone traps (5/acre) to monitor adult moths.

**Organic:** Release egg parasitoid Trichogramma japonicum @ 1 lakh/acre at 3 releases. Collect and destroy egg masses from leaves.

### 1.3 Bollworm in Cotton (Helicoverpa armigera)

American Bollworm is the most damaging cotton pest in AP (Kurnool, Prakasam districts).

| Chemical | Trade Name | Dose | Waiting Period |
|----------|-----------|------|----------------|
| Emamectin benzoate 5SG | Proclaim | 0.4g/L | 14 days |
| Indoxacarb 14.5SC | Avaunt | 1ml/L | 14 days |
| Spinosad 45SC | Tracer | 0.3ml/L | 7 days |
| Thiodicarb 75WP | Larvin | 1.5g/L | 14 days |
| Chlorantraniliprole 18.5SC | Coragen | 0.3ml/L | 7 days |

**Resistance management:** Rotate chemicals. Never spray same chemical more than 2 consecutive times. Avoid Cypermethrin, Deltamethrin alone (resistance reported).

**Pheromone traps:** Use Helilure (Helicoverpa pheromone) traps @ 5/acre. Replace lure every 3-4 weeks. Economic Threshold Level (ETL) = 8 moths/trap/week = start spraying.

**Organic:** Spray Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) @ 1kg/acre when larvae are small (1st-2nd instar). Very effective and safe. Apply in evening.

### 1.5 Whitefly (Bemisia tabaci) - Vector of TYLCV Virus

Whitefly is critical to manage in tomato because it spreads TYLCV leaf curl virus.

| Chemical | Trade Name | Dose | Waiting Period |
|----------|-----------|------|----------------|
| Imidacloprid 17.8SL | Confidor | 0.3ml/L | 21 days |
| Thiamethoxam 25WG | Actara | 0.25g/L | 14 days |
| Buprofezin 25SC | Applaud | 1.5ml/L | 14 days |
| Spiromesifen 22.9SC | Oberon | 1ml/L | 7 days |
| Diafenthiuron 50WP | Polo | 1.5g/L | 7 days |

**Nursery treatment:** Drench seedlings with Imidacloprid 0.3ml/L one week before transplanting. This protects early growth (systemic action for 3-4 weeks).

**Organic:** Yellow sticky traps (40/acre) to capture adults. Neem oil 3ml/L + 0.5ml/L soap solution spray on leaf undersides. Silver/reflective mulch repels whiteflies strongly.

### 1.6 Thrips in Chilli, Onion, Cotton

Thrips cause silver streaking on leaves, scarred fruit, and spread TSWV virus in chilli.

| Chemical | Trade Name | Dose | Waiting Period |
|----------|-----------|------|----------------|
| Fipronil 5SC | Regent | 1.5ml/L | 30 days |
| Spinosad 45SC | Tracer | 0.3ml/L | 7 days |
| Imidacloprid 17.8SL | Confidor | 0.3ml/L | 21 days |
| Acephate 75SP | Starthene | 1.5g/L | 14 days |
| Thiamethoxam 25WG | Actara | 0.25g/L | 14 days |

**Organic:** Blue sticky traps for thrips monitoring. Neem oil 3ml/L spray. Avoid planting near onion/garlic which harbor thrips.

### 1.7 Spider Mites in Tomato, Chilli, Brinjal

Mites explode in summer. Look for fine webbing and stippling on leaf underside.

| Chemical | Trade Name | Dose | Waiting Period |
|----------|-----------|------|----------------|
| Dicofol 18.5EC | Kelthane | 2.5ml/L | 14 days |
| Abamectin 1.8EC | Vertimec | 0.5ml/L | 7 days |
| Spiromesifen 22.9SC | Oberon | 1ml/L | 7 days |
| Propargite 57EC | Omite | 2ml/L | 7 days |
| Hexythiazox 5EC | Nissorun | 1ml/L | 7 days |

**IMPORTANT:** Do NOT use pyrethroid (Cypermethrin, Deltamethrin, Fenvalerate) for mites - they worsen mite outbreaks by killing natural enemies.

**Organic:** Forceful water sprays on leaf undersides to dislodge mites. Neem oil 3ml/L + wetting agent. Maintain field moisture (dry conditions favor mites).

## SECTION 5: BANNED PESTICIDES IN INDIA

**DO NOT USE - THESE ARE BANNED:**
- Endosulfan (banned 2011) - highly toxic, causes neurological damage
- Monocrotophos - BANNED on vegetables and fruits; extreme bird and mammal toxicity
- Methyl parathion - banned for most crops
- Phorate - restricted; do not use on vegetables
- Chlordane, DDT, Aldrin - banned long ago; soil persistent

**ACTION:** If a dealer tries to sell banned chemicals, report to District Agriculture Officer.

## SECTION 6: SAFETY PRECAUTIONS

### Before Spraying
1. Read the label fully in Telugu/English
2. Wear: rubber gloves, mask/respirator, full-sleeve clothes, rubber boots
3. Never spray alone - have someone nearby
4. Check wind direction - spray downwind
5. Do NOT spray in strong wind (causes drift) or rain

### While Spraying
1. Never eat, drink, or smoke while spraying
2. Avoid touching face or eyes
3. Keep children, women (especially pregnant), and animals away from field
4. Wash sprayer nozzle blockage with a stick - NEVER with mouth

### After Spraying
1. Wash hands and face with soap immediately after
2. Wash all clothing with soap separately
3. Bathe before eating
4. Clean sprayer thoroughly after each use
5. Store chemicals in locked place away from food and children

### First Aid for Pesticide Poisoning
- Skin contact: Wash immediately with soap and water for 15 minutes
- Eye contact: Wash with clean water for 15-20 minutes
- Ingestion: Do NOT induce vomiting. Take to hospital immediately. Carry the pesticide container.
- Emergency: Call 1800-425-1551 (AP Agriculture Helpline) or go to nearest PHC
