# Paddy (Rice) Cultivation Guide – Andhra Pradesh

## Introduction
Paddy is the **most important staple crop** of Andhra Pradesh, cultivated across coastal, delta, and upland regions. AP is one of India's leading rice-producing states, with the Krishna-Godavari delta belt being the primary cultivation zone.

## Recommended Varieties for AP
| Variety | Type | Duration | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|---|
| **BPT 5204** (Samba Masuri) | Fine grain | 135–140 days | Most popular, premium quality |
| **MTU 7029** (Swarna Sub1) | Medium grain | 125–130 days | Flood-tolerant version of Swarna |
| **Swarna (MTU 7029)** | Medium grain | 140–145 days | High yield, widely grown |
| **NLR 34449** | Medium | 130–135 days | AP-specific release, blast resistant |
| **MTU 1010** | Fine grain | 120–125 days | Short duration, good quality |
| **Improved Samba Masuri** | Fine | 140 days | Disease-resistant BPT 5204 derivative |

## Cropping Seasons
| Season | Sowing Time | Transplanting | Harvest |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Kharif (Vanakalam)** | June – July | July – August | October – November |
| **Rabi (Dalwa)** | November – December | December – January | March – April |

## Soil & Field Preparation
- **Best soil:** Heavy clay loam soils with good water retention
- Deep ploughing (20–25 cm) followed by 2–3 harrowing
- Puddling for transplanted paddy (improves water retention, reduces weeds)
- Levelling is critical – ensures uniform water distribution
- Maintain **bunds** around the field to retain water

## Seed Rate & Seed Treatment
- **Seed rate:** 25–30 kg/ha (transplanted); 80–100 kg/ha (direct seeded)
- **Seed Treatment:**
  1. Salt water test – float test to remove empty/light seeds (1 kg salt in 9 litres water)
  2. Soak seeds 24 hours in clean water
  3. Treat with **Carbendazim 2g/kg** seed or **Trichoderma** for disease protection
  4. Incubate 24–36 hours until sprouting

## Nursery Preparation
- Prepare raised nursery beds (1 m wide, 10–15 m long)
- Apply well-rotted FYM (2 kg/m²)
- Sow pre-germinated seeds uniformly at 100g/m²
- Nursery age at transplanting: **25–30 days** (2–3 leaf stage)

## Transplanting
- **Spacing:** 20 × 15 cm (recommended); up to 25 × 20 cm for semi-dry areas
- **Seedlings per hill:** 2–3 seedlings
- Transplant in the morning or evening to reduce stress
- Transplant at 3–5 cm depth

## Water Management
- Maintain **2–5 cm standing water** throughout crop growth
- **Alternate Wetting and Drying (AWD)** can save 20–30% water without yield loss
- Critical water requirement stages:
  - Tillering (21–35 days)
  - Panicle initiation (55–65 days)
  - Flowering and grain filling
- Drain field 10–15 days before harvest to facilitate harvesting

## Fertilizer Schedule
**Recommended Dose:** NPK = 120:60:60 kg/ha

| Nutrient | Total Dose | Application |
|---|---|---|
| Nitrogen (Urea) | 120 kg/ha | Split: 1/3 basal + 1/3 at tillering + 1/3 at PI |
| Phosphorus (SSP/DAP) | 60 kg/ha | Full basal |
| Potassium (MOP) | 60 kg/ha | Half basal + half at PI |
| Zinc Sulphate | 25 kg/ha | Basal (if deficiency observed) |

## Weed Management
- **Manual weeding:** At 20 and 40 days after transplanting
- **Herbicides:**
  - Pre-emergence: Pretilachlor 0.75 kg a.i./ha (within 3 days of transplanting)
  - Post-emergence: Bispyribac sodium 25g a.i./ha (at 15–20 DAT)

## Pest Management
| Pest | Damage | Control |
|---|---|---|
| **Brown Plant Hopper (BPH)** | Sucks sap, causes hopper burn | Buprofezin 25SC @ 1ml/L or Thiamethoxam 25WG @ 0.2g/L |
| **Yellow Stem Borer** | Dead hearts, white ears | Chlorpyrifos 20EC @ 2.5 ml/L; release Trichogramma @ 2.5 lakh/ha |
| **Leaf Folder** | Rolls and feeds on leaves | Chlorantraniliprole 18.5SC @ 0.3 ml/L |
| **Gall Midge** | Silver shoot (dead heart) | Carbofuran 3G @ 25 kg/ha soil application |

## Disease Management
| Disease | Symptoms | Control |
|---|---|---|
| **Blast (Pyricularia oryzae)** | Diamond-shaped lesions on leaves/neck | Tricyclazole 75WP @ 0.6g/L; use resistant varieties |
| **Bacterial Blight (BB)** | Water-soaked leaf margin, yellowing | Copper oxychloride 3g/L; avoid excess nitrogen |
| **Sheath Blight** | Oval lesions on sheath | Validamycin 3L @ 2ml/L; Hexaconazole 5SC @ 2ml/L |
| **Brown Spot** | Oval brown spots | Mancozeb 75WP @ 2.5g/L |

## Harvest
- **Harvest when:** 80% of grains turn golden/straw coloured
- Approximately 30–35 days after flowering
- Moisture content at harvest: 20–22% (ideal for combine; 25% for manual)
- Thresh immediately after cutting to reduce shattering loss

## Expected Yield
- **Irrigated (Transplanted):** 5–7 tonnes/ha
- **Direct Seeded:** 4–5 tonnes/ha
- **BPT 5204 (Quality rice):** 5–5.5 tonnes/ha under good management
