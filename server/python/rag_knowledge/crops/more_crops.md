# Andhra Pradesh — Major Crops Quick Reference
# Source: ANGRAU, ICAR, AP Agriculture Department

## Maize (Corn) — Zea mays

### AP Varieties
NMH 803, DKC 9141, HQPM 1 (quality protein maize), Navjot, Vivek QPM-9

### Season & Sowing
- Kharif: June–July (main season in AP)
- Rabi: October–November (irrigated)

### Fertilizer
NPK: 180:80:60 kg/ha. Apply N in 3 splits: basal, knee-high (25 days), tasseling (45 days).

### Water Requirement
550–700mm. Critical stages: germination, knee-high, tasseling, grain fill. Avoid waterlogging.

### Common Pests & Diseases
- Fall Armyworm (Spodoptera frugiperda): spray Emamectin benzoate 0.4g/L at whorl stage
- Gray leaf spot: apply Propiconazole 1ml/L
- Common rust: resistant varieties; Mancozeb 2.5g/L
- Northern leaf blight: Carbendazim 1g/L

### Yield
6–8 tonnes/ha irrigated; 4–5 tonnes/ha rainfed

## Sugarcane — Saccharum officinarum

### AP Varieties
87A298, Co 86032, CoA 92081, NW 5260

### Season & Planting
October–November (main planting), February–March (ratoon)

### Fertilizer
NPK: 300:120:120 kg/ha over 12 months. Apply in 4 splits.

### Water Requirement
1500–2500mm/year. Requires regular irrigation every 7–10 days.

### Pest & Disease Management
- Borers: Chlorpyrifos 2.5ml/L
- Smut: use disease-free setts, hot water treatment 50°C for 2.5 hours
- Red rot: avoid waterlogging, destroy infected clumps

### Yield
80–100 tonnes/ha

## Onion — Allium cepa

### AP Varieties
Pusa Red, Agrifound Dark Red, N-53, Arka Niketan

### Season
Kharif: June–July; Rabi: October–November (main AP crop)

### Fertilizer
NPK: 100:50:50 kg/ha. Sulfur 20kg/ha improves pungency and shelf life.

### Water Requirement
350–550mm. Irrigate every 8–10 days. Stop 10 days before harvest.

### Pest & Disease
- Thrips: Spinosad 0.3ml/L or Fipronil 2ml/L
- Purple blotch: Mancozeb 2.5g/L + Iprodione 1.5g/L
- Basal rot: soil drench with Carbendazim

### Yield
20–25 tonnes/ha

## Brinjal (Eggplant) — Solanum melongena

### AP Varieties
Pusa Purple Long, Arka Sheel, Swarna Pratibha, Manjari Gota

### Season
Year-round in AP with irrigation; main season June–July

### Fertilizer
NPK: 120:60:60 kg/ha

### Pest & Disease
- Shoot and fruit borer (Leucinodes orbonalis): most serious pest
  - Use pheromone traps (5/acre), remove infested shoot tips
  - Spray Emamectin benzoate 0.4g/L or Spinosad 0.3ml/L
- Little leaf (phytoplasma): remove infected plants, control leafhoppers
- Damping off: Thiram seed treatment 3g/kg seed

### Yield
25–35 tonnes/ha

## Groundnut — Arachis hypogaea

### AP Varieties
K-6, ICGS 76, Dharani, Narayani, Kadiri 9

### Season
Kharif: June–July; Rabi: November–December (irrigated)

### Key Requirement
Gypsum (calcium source) is essential — apply 500kg/ha at pegging stage.
Without calcium, empty pods (pops) increase significantly.

### Fertilizer
NPK: 25:50:0 kg/ha (nodule fixation provides N)
Boron 1kg/ha improves pod filling

### Water Requirement
500–600mm. Very sensitive to waterlogging — ensure good drainage.

### Pest & Disease
- Leaf miner: Quinalphos 2ml/L
- Tikka (early/late leaf spot): Mancozeb 2.5g/L
- Rust: Chlorothalonil 2g/L
- Collar rot: Trichoderma seed treatment + soil application

### Yield
Pods: 2–2.5 tonnes/ha; Kernel recovery 70–73%

## Mango — Mangifera indica

### AP Varieties
Banganapalli (Safeda), Totapuri, Himayat (Imam Pasand), Alphonso (limited), Kesar, Raspuri

### Flowering & Harvest
Flowering: December–January; Harvest: April–June

### Fertilizer
Per tree/year (mature tree): N:250g, P:125g, K:500g + FYM 50kg

### Common Problems
- Mango hopper: Imidacloprid 0.3ml/L at pre-flowering
- Powdery mildew: Wettable sulfur 2g/L or Hexaconazole 1ml/L
- Anthracnose: Carbendazim 1g/L
- Malformation: prune infected panicles + spray NAA 200ppm

### Yield
100–200 kg/tree at maturity (15+ years)

## Banana — Musa paradisiaca

### AP Varieties
Cavendish (Grand Naine), Robusta, Poovan, Red Banana, Nendran

### Season
Year-round planting in AP; main harvest 12–14 months after planting

### Fertilizer per plant
N:200g, P:60g, K:300g in 4-6 splits. Fertigate through drip if available.

### Water Requirement
1200–1800mm. Drip irrigation recommended (60% water saving).

### Pest & Disease
- Panama wilt (Fusarium oxysporum): no cure — use resistant varieties
- Sigatoka leaf spot: Propiconazole 1ml/L + Mancozeb 2.5g/L
- Bunchy top virus: controlled by aphid vector — Dimethoate 2ml/L

### Yield
20–35 tonnes/ha

## Turmeric — Curcuma longa

### AP Varieties
Duggirala, Tekurpeta, Sugandham, Erode, Salem

### Season
May–June planting; harvest February–March (9 months)

### Fertilizer
NPK: 60:50:120 kg/ha. Earthing up at 45 days.

### Water Requirement
1500mm. Needs well-distributed moisture. Mulch with dry leaves.

### Pest & Disease
- Rhizome rot: Metalaxyl+Mancozeb 2.5g/L drench
- Leaf blotch: Mancozeb 2.5g/L
- Shoot borer: Carbaryl 4g/L at 45-day intervals

### Yield
Fresh rhizomes: 25–30 tonnes/ha; Dry recovery: 20–25%

## Papaya — Carica papaya

### AP Varieties
Red Lady (Taiwan), CO-7, Pusa Nanha, Pusa Dwarf

### Season
Year-round; plant June–July or October–November

### Fertilizer
N:250g, P:250g, K:500g per plant per year in monthly splits

### Common Problems
- Papaya ring spot virus (PRSV): most destructive; no cure
  - Control aphid vectors with Dimethoate 2ml/L
  - Use virus-tested seedlings only
- Phytophthora root rot: avoid waterlogging, Metalaxyl drench
- Powdery mildew: Wettable sulfur 2g/L

### Yield
50–80 kg/plant over 2 years

## Tobacco — Nicotiana tabacum

### AP Varieties
(Flue-cured) PVII, K-326, FC-446; (Bidi) Dhanavantri, Lanka

### Season
Nursery: June–July; Transplanting: September–October

### Special Management
- Topping: remove terminal bud at 10–12 leaves for FCV; 16–18 for Bidi
- Sucker control: apply MH (Maleic hydrazide) 2500 ppm after topping

### Yield
FCV: 2000–2500 kg cured leaf/ha
