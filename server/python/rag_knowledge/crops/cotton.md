# Cotton Cultivation Guide — Andhra Pradesh
# Source: ANGRAU, ICAR, AP Agriculture Department

## Overview
Cotton is a major cash crop in northern AP (Kurnool, Nandyal, Anantapur, Prakasam districts).
AP grows both Bt hybrid cotton and non-Bt varieties.

## Recommended Varieties
| Variety | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| NCS 145 | Bt Hybrid | High yielding, bollworm tolerant |
| Bunny Bt | Bt Hybrid | Popular in AP, dual-gene |
| RCH 2 | Bt Hybrid | Good fiber quality |
| MRC 7017 | Bt Hybrid | Drought tolerant |

## Season & Sowing
- Main season: June–July (Kharif)
- Sowing after first good rains (soil temp >25°C)
- Seed rate: 2.5 kg/ha (hybrid), 10–12 kg/ha (desi)

## Soil
Deep black cotton soil (vertisols) or red loamy soils. pH 6.5–8.0. Good drainage essential.

## Spacing
- Irrigated: 90×60 cm or 75×45 cm
- Rainfed: 90×60 cm

## Fertilizer Schedule
NPK: 180:90:90 kg/ha
| Stage | Nutrient | Dose |
|---|---|---|
| Basal | P + K full dose + 25% N | At sowing |
| 30 DAS | 25% N | Squaring |
| 60 DAS | 25% N | Flowering |
| 90 DAS | 25% N | Boll development |
Sulfur 40kg/ha at basal improves fiber quality.
Boron 1kg/ha spray at bud initiation reduces boll shedding.

## Irrigation
- Total: 700–900mm
- Critical stages: germination, squaring (50 DAS), flowering (70 DAS), boll development (90 DAS)
- Avoid waterlogging — leads to root rot and wilt

## Pest Management
### Major Pests
**Bollworms (Pink, American, Spotted)**
- Monitoring: Install pheromone traps (5/acre)
- Spray threshold: 5–8 larvae/100 plants
- Chemical: Emamectin benzoate 0.5g/L, Indoxacarb 1ml/L, Spinosad 0.3ml/L

**Whitefly (Bemisia tabaci) — Vector of Cotton Leaf Curl Virus**
- Monitoring: Yellow sticky traps
- Chemical: Imidacloprid 0.3ml/L (avoid repeated use — resistance)
- Alternate with: Spiromesifen 0.9ml/L or Pyriproxyfen 1ml/L

**Thrips**
- Spray: Fipronil 2ml/L or Spinosad 0.3ml/L at seedling stage

**Jassids (Leafhoppers)**
- Spray: Dimethoate 2ml/L

**Mealybug (Phenacoccus solenopsis)**
- Remove and destroy infested plants/branches
- Spray: Profenofos 2ml/L or Chlorpyrifos 2.5ml/L
- Biological: Release Cryptolaemus montrouzieri (mealybug destroyer)

## Disease Management

**Fusarium Wilt (Fusarium oxysporum)**
- Symptoms: Yellowing and wilting of lower leaves, dark discoloration inside stem
- Management: Seed treatment with Carboxin 2g/kg. Use resistant varieties. Soil drench with Carbendazim 1g/L.

**Root Rot (Rhizoctonia solani)**
- Symptoms: Damping off at seedling stage, brown discoloration at base
- Management: Thiram or Captan seed treatment 3g/kg seed. Avoid waterlogging.

**Grey Mildew (Ramularia areola)**
- Symptoms: White powdery patches on undersurface of leaves
- Management: Spray Carbendazim 1g/L + Mancozeb 2g/L

**Cotton Leaf Curl Virus (CLCuV)**
- Vector: Whitefly
- Symptoms: Leaf curling, thickening of veins, enations on leaf undersurface
- Management: Control whitefly, uproot and destroy infected plants, avoid late sowing

## Harvesting
- Harvest when 60% bolls open
- Pick in 3–4 pickings at 10–15 day intervals
- First picking gives highest quality fiber

## Yield
- Bt hybrid: 2.5–3.5 tonnes seed cotton/ha (800–1200kg lint)
- Traditional: 1.5–2 tonnes/ha

## Post-Harvest
- Ginning ratio (lint/seed cotton): 33–37%
- Store in dry, cool conditions, moisture <9%
