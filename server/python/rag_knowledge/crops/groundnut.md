# Groundnut Cultivation Guide – Andhra Pradesh

## Introduction
Groundnut (peanut) is the **most important oilseed crop** of Andhra Pradesh. Anantapur district is the **world's largest single-district groundnut producer**. Kurnool, Kadapa, Chittoor, Prakasam, and Nellore are other major districts. AP contributes nearly 20–25% of India's groundnut production. The crop is mainly rainfed but gives better yields under irrigation.

## Recommended Varieties for AP
| Variety | Duration | Type | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|---|
| **K-6 (Kadiri-6)** | 100–105 days | Virginia bunch | High yield, popular in AP |
| **ICGS 76** | 105–110 days | Spanish bunch | High shelling %, drought tolerant |
| **Dharani (TCGS 29)** | 95–100 days | Spanish bunch | Short duration, good for late planting |
| **Narayani (ICGS 44)** | 100–105 days | Spanish bunch | Large kernel, dual purpose |
| **Kadiri-9** | 105–110 days | Virginia | Very high yield potential |
| **TG-37A** | 120–125 days | Virginia | Widely grown in Anantapur |

## Cropping Season
| Season | Sowing Time | Harvest |
|---|---|---|
| **Kharif (main)** | June – July | September – October |
| **Rabi/Summer (irrigated)** | January – February | April – May |

- Kharif crop is the primary season under rainfed conditions.

## Soil Requirements
- **Best soil:** Sandy loam and loamy soils with good drainage
- Red soils of Rayalaseema are ideal for groundnut
- **pH:** 6.0 – 7.0 (slightly acidic to neutral)
- Avoid heavy clay or waterlogged soils (causes pod rot and poor peg penetration)
- Calcium in soil must be adequate (critical for pod filling)

## Land Preparation
- Deep ploughing (25–30 cm) to loosen the soil for peg penetration
- 2–3 cross harrowing to fine tilth
- Form ridges and furrows (30 × 10 cm) or flat beds
- Apply 5–10 tonnes/ha **FYM** at last ploughing

## Seed Rate & Preparation
- **Seed rate:** 100–120 kg/ha (shelled seeds)
- Select bold, healthy kernels; reject shriveled/discolored seeds
- **Seed treatment (in order):**
  1. Thiram 3g/kg + Carbendazim 2g/kg (fungicide treatment)
  2. **Rhizobium culture** inoculation (250g/10 kg seed) – fixes atmospheric nitrogen
  3. **PSB (Phosphate Solubilizing Bacteria)** culture – improves P availability
- Allow treated seeds to dry in shade for 30 min before sowing

## Sowing & Spacing
- **Spacing:** 30 × 10 cm (row × plant)
- **Sowing depth:** 5–7 cm
- Ensure good soil moisture at sowing for uniform germination
- Sow with seed drill or manually on ridges

## Fertilizer Schedule
**Recommended dose:** NPK = 25:50:0 kg/ha + Gypsum

| Input | Quantity | Application Method |
|---|---|---|
| Nitrogen (Urea) | 25 kg N/ha (55 kg Urea) | Full as basal |
| Phosphorus (SSP) | 50 kg P₂O₅/ha (310 kg SSP) | Full as basal |
| Potassium | Not required in most AP soils | Only if deficient |
| **Gypsum** | **500 kg/ha** | **Applied at pegging stage (35–40 DAS)** |
| Zinc Sulphate | 25 kg/ha | Basal if Zn deficiency observed |

> **Gypsum is critical for AP groundnut** – calcium from gypsum is directly absorbed by pods at pegging stage, improving pod filling and kernel quality.

## Irrigation Management
- **Rainfed crop:** Ensure proper ridges and furrows for in-situ moisture conservation
- **Critical irrigation stages:**
  - Pegging (35–40 DAS) – most critical
  - Pod development (60–75 DAS)
  - Pod filling / kernel development (80–95 DAS)
- Irrigate at **field capacity** at the peg zone – avoid waterlogging
- **Total irrigations:** 4–5 life-saving irrigations under rainfed drought; 6–8 for irrigated
- **Avoid irrigation 15 days before harvest** to facilitate easy pod pulling

## Weed Management
- **Pre-emergence herbicide:** Pendimethalin 30EC @ 3.3 L/ha (within 2 days of sowing)
- **Hand weeding:** 25 and 45 DAS (especially before and after pegging)
- **Inter-cultivation:** Blind hoeing at 15 DAS; careful hoeing between rows at 30 DAS
- Do NOT hoe after pegging – will damage pegs and pods

## Pest Management
| Pest | Damage Symptom | Control Measure |
|---|---|---|
| **Leaf Miner** (*Aproaerema modicella*) | Blotch-mining of leaflets; rolled, brown leaves | Monocrotophos 36SL @ 1.5ml/L; Quinalphos 25EC @ 2ml/L |
| **Thrips** | Leaf curl, silvery stippling | Dimethoate 30EC @ 2ml/L; Fipronil 5SC @ 1.5ml/L |
| **White Grub** (soil pest) | Roots/pods damaged underground | Phorate 10G @ 10kg/ha (at sowing); Chlorpyrifos soil drench |
| **Spodoptera** (leaf eating caterpillar) | Large irregular holes in leaves | Emamectin benzoate 5SG @ 0.4g/L; Bacillus thuringiensis 1g/L |
| **Termites** | Plant wilting, stem/root damage | Chlorpyrifos 20EC @ 5ml/L soil drench |

## Disease Management
| Disease | Symptoms | Control |
|---|---|---|
| **Early Leaf Spot** (*Cercospora arachidicola*) – Tikka | Circular brown spots with yellow halo, upper surface | Mancozeb 75WP @ 2.5g/L @ 30, 45, 60 DAS |
| **Late Leaf Spot** (*Cercosporidium personatum*) | Dark chocolate-brown spots on lower surface | Chlorothalonil 75WP @ 2g/L; Carbendazim 1g/L |
| **Rust** (*Puccinia arachidis*) | Orange-brown pustules on underside of leaves | Mancozeb 2.5g/L; Hexaconazole 5SC @ 1ml/L |
| **Collar Rot** (*Aspergillus niger*) | Black cottony growth at stem base, seedling death | Carbendazim seed treatment; avoid waterlogging; Copper oxychloride 3g/L drench |
| **Crown Rot / Stem Rot** | White mycelial growth at stem base | Trichoderma soil application; Carbendazim 2g/L |
| **Bud Necrosis** (TSWV) | Growing tip browning, stunting | Control thrips vector; Imidacloprid spray |

## Aflatoxin Management
- Aflatoxin (produced by *Aspergillus flavus*) is a serious quality concern in AP groundnut
- **Prevention measures:**
  - Harvest at proper maturity – avoid delayed harvest
  - Dry pods quickly to below 8–9% moisture
  - Store in clean, dry, well-ventilated gunny bags
  - Avoid mechanical damage during threshing
  - Use **biocontrol agent Aflasafe** in field where applicable
- Aflatoxin-contaminated groundnut is rejected by export buyers – proper drying is critical

## Harvest
- **Harvest indicators:**
  - Foliage turns yellow and plant starts drying
  - Inner shell shows **dark brown/black veins** (reticulate marking)
  - Outer shell is hard; kernel fills the shell completely
  - Typical harvest: **100–110 DAS** (variety-dependent)
- Uproot plants manually or use groundnut digger
- Stack plants for 3–4 days for wilting
- **Thresh** by beating bunches or using mechanical thresher
- **Dry pods** under sun to reach safe moisture content (8–9%) for storage

## Expected Yield
- **Rainfed (Kharif):** 1.5–2 tonnes pods/ha
- **Irrigated:** 2–2.5 tonnes pods/ha
- **Kernel yield:** Approximately 70–75% of pod weight

## Key Tips for AP Farmers
- Treat seeds with Rhizobium – saves 50% nitrogen fertilizer cost
- Apply gypsum at pegging without fail – this is the single most yield-impacting practice
- Rotate with paddy or cotton to break pest and disease cycles
- Sell in APMC market (Anantapur, Nandyal, Markapur) after proper grading for best price
