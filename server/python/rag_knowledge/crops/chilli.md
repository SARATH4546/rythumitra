# Chilli Cultivation Guide – Andhra Pradesh

## Introduction
Andhra Pradesh is the **largest chilli-producing state in India**, contributing over 50% of national production. Guntur, Khammam (Bhadradri), Krishna, and Prakasam are the primary chilli-growing districts. Guntur chilli is world-famous for its pungency and color. Chilli is grown both for fresh/green market and dry red chilli export.

## Recommended Varieties for AP
| Variety | Type | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **LCA 305** (Guntur Sannam) | Open Pollinated | High pungency, deep red color, widely exported |
| **Guntur Sannam S4** | Selection | Standard commercial variety, high oleoresin |
| **Byadgi Kaddi** | Open Pollinated | Mild pungency, high color (paprika), Karnataka/AP border |
| **Jwala** | Open Pollinated | Very pungent, small thin fruits |
| **G4** | Hybrid | High yield, virus-tolerant |
| **Mahyco MHC 11** | Hybrid | Suitable for Kharif, tolerant to CLCV |

## Cropping Seasons
| Season | Nursery Sowing | Transplanting | Harvest |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Kharif** | May – June | June – July | October – January |
| **Rabi** | September | October – November | February – May |

- Rabi crop generally gives better quality dry chilli with good color and pungency.

## Nursery Preparation
- **Seed requirement:** 500 g/ha
- Prepare raised beds: 1 m × 3 m size, 15 cm raised
- Soil mixture: FYM + Neem cake + Garden soil (2:1:7)
- Drench nursery beds with **Carbendazim 2g/L** + **Copper oxychloride 3g/L** before sowing
- Cover with shade net (50%) after sowing
- **Transplanting age:** 5–6 weeks (35–40 days), seedlings with 6–8 leaves

## Transplanting
- **Spacing:** 45 × 30 cm (row × plant distance)
- Dip roots of seedlings in **Trichoderma viride** slurry before transplanting
- Spray **Imidacloprid 17.8 SL @ 0.3 ml/L** 1 day before transplanting (thrips/virus control)
- Transplant in evening; irrigate immediately

## Irrigation
- **Drip irrigation recommended** – Saves 30–40% water, reduces humidity-related diseases
- Irrigate at 3–4 day intervals in summer; 7–10 days in Rabi
- Critical stages: Transplant establishment, flower bud initiation, flowering, fruit development
- Avoid overhead irrigation during flowering (causes fruit drop)

## Fertilizer Schedule
**Recommended dose:** NPK = 120:60:40 kg/ha

| Application Stage | Nutrients |
|---|---|
| Basal (before transplanting) | Full P (60 kg/ha) + 1/3 N + Full K (40 kg/ha) |
| 30 DAT (active growth) | 1/3 N |
| 60 DAT (flowering/fruiting) | 1/3 N |

- Spray **Boron 0.2%** at flowering stage to improve fruit set
- Apply **Magnesium Sulphate 1%** if leaf yellowing observed (interveinal chlorosis)
- Fertigate through drip using 19:19:19 and 12:61:0 combinations at appropriate stages

## Pest Management
| Pest | Damage Symptom | Control Measure |
|---|---|---|
| **Thrips** (*Scirtothrips dorsalis*) | Silvery upward leaf curling, flower drop | Fipronil 5SC @ 1.5ml/L; Acephate 75SP @ 1.5g/L; Blue sticky traps |
| **Mites** (Yellow Mite, Red Spider) | Bronzing, downward leaf curl, webbing | Abamectin 1.9EC @ 0.5ml/L; Spiromesifen 22.9SC @ 1ml/L |
| **Aphids** | Sticky honeydew, sooty mould, virus spread | Dimethoate 30EC @ 2ml/L; Neem oil 5ml/L |
| **Fruit Borer** (*Helicoverpa/Spodoptera*) | Bored fruits, larva inside | Emamectin benzoate 5SG @ 0.4g/L; Chlorantraniliprole 18.5SC @ 0.3ml/L |
| **Mealy Bug** | White cottony mass, stunted plants | Profenofos+Cypermethrin @ 2ml/L |

## Disease Management
| Disease | Symptoms | Control |
|---|---|---|
| **Leaf Curl Virus (ChiLCV)** | Upward curling, yellowing, stunting | Control thrips vector; remove infected plants; use resistant varieties; Imidacloprid |
| **Anthracnose** (*Colletotrichum*) | Sunken dark lesions on ripe fruits | Mancozeb 2.5g/L + Carbendazim 1g/L; Azoxystrobin 1ml/L |
| **Powdery Mildew** | White powdery growth on leaves | Wettable Sulphur 80WP @ 3g/L; Myclobutanil 10WP @ 1g/L |
| **Phytophthora Blight** (*P. capsici*) | Sudden wilting, collar rot, fruit rot | Metalaxyl+Mancozeb @ 2.5g/L drench + spray; improve drainage |
| **Damping Off** (Nursery) | Seedling collapse at base | Carbendazim 2g/L drench in nursery |
| **Cercospora Leaf Spot** | Circular brown spots, leaf fall | Copper oxychloride 3g/L |

## Spray Schedule Recommendation
- **Nursery:** Carbendazim + Copper oxychloride drench before sowing
- **Weeks 1–3 (post-transplant):** Imidacloprid 0.3ml/L for thrips/virus management
- **Weeks 4–6:** Mancozeb 2.5g/L + Abamectin 0.5ml/L (disease + mite)
- **Weeks 7–9 (flowering):** Emamectin 0.4g/L + Azoxystrobin 1ml/L
- **Weeks 10–12 (fruiting):** Chlorantraniliprole 0.3ml/L + Copper oxychloride 3g/L
- Observe **PHI (Pre-Harvest Interval)** strictly – minimum 7 days before harvest

## Harvest
- **Green chilli:** Harvest when fruits are fully grown, firm, and bright green (50–60 DAT)
- **Red chilli (dry):** Harvest when **70% or more fruits turn red** on the plant
- Pick in rounds every 7–10 days
- **Dry chilli immediately** after harvest under sun (target 8–10% moisture content)
- **Avoid rain exposure** during drying – causes mold and color loss

## Expected Yield
- **Green chilli:** 10–15 tonnes/ha
- **Dry red chilli:** 2.5–3 tonnes/ha (OPs); 3–4 tonnes/ha (hybrids)
- **LCA 305 / Guntur Sannam:** 2.5–3 tonnes dry/ha

## Market & Storage Tips
- Guntur APMC is the **largest chilli market in Asia** – grade chilli before selling
- Store dried chilli in gunny bags in well-ventilated, moisture-free godowns
- Grading: Super, A-grade, B-grade (based on color, pungency, skin quality)
