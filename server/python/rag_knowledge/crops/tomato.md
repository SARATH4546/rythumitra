# Tomato Cultivation Guide – Andhra Pradesh

## Introduction
Tomato is one of the most important vegetable crops in Andhra Pradesh, cultivated extensively in Kurnool, Chittoor, Kadapa, Guntur, and Krishna districts. AP is a major tomato-supplying state to national markets. The crop requires careful pest and disease management for profitable cultivation.

## Recommended Varieties
| Variety | Type | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Naveen** | Open pollinated | Early maturing, good shelf life |
| **US 440** | Hybrid | High yield, firm fruits, suitable for transport |
| **Arka Rakshak** | Hybrid (IIHR) | Multi-disease resistant (ToLCV, bacterial wilt, early blight) |
| **Arka Samrat** | Hybrid (IIHR) | High lycopene, processing quality |
| **Pusa Rohini** | Open pollinated | Suitable for Rabi season |
| **CO 3** | Open pollinated | Suitable for summer cultivation |

## Cropping Seasons in AP
| Season | Nursery Sowing | Transplanting | Harvest |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Kharif** | May – June | June – July | September – November |
| **Rabi** | September – October | October – November | January – March |
| **Summer** | January – February | February – March | April – June |

## Nursery Preparation
- **Seed requirement:** 400–500 g/ha (25 g per 100 m² nursery area)
- Prepare raised nursery beds: 1 m wide × 3 m long
- Soil mixture: Garden soil + FYM + Sand (1:1:1)
- **Drench nursery** with Carbendazim 1g/L + Copper oxychloride 3g/L before sowing
- Cover with dry grass/shade net after sowing
- Transplant seedlings at **25–30 days** (4–6 true leaf stage)
- Spray Imidacloprid 0.3 ml/L one day before transplanting to prevent virus spread

## Field Preparation & Transplanting
- Deep ploughing, 2–3 harrowing, and fine tilth
- Form ridges and furrows or flat beds with drip irrigation
- **Spacing:** 60 × 45 cm (row × plant)
- Apply basal FYM: 25–30 tonnes/ha
- Transplant in evening hours; water immediately after transplanting

## Irrigation
- **Drip irrigation preferred** – saves 40–50% water, reduces soil moisture diseases
- Critical irrigation stages: Transplanting establishment, flowering, fruit set, fruit development
- **Avoid overhead irrigation** during flowering and fruiting (increases disease incidence)
- Irrigation frequency: Every 3–4 days in summer; 5–7 days in Rabi

## Fertilizer Schedule
**Recommended dose:** NPK = 180:120:100 kg/ha

| Stage | Fertilizer Application |
|---|---|
| Basal (at transplanting) | Full P, 1/4 N, 1/2 K + 25t FYM/ha |
| 15 days after transplanting | 1/4 N + micronutrient spray |
| Flowering stage (30–35 DAT) | 1/4 N + 1/4 K |
| Fruit development (50–55 DAT) | 1/4 N + 1/4 K |

- Apply **Calcium Nitrate 1% spray** at fruit setting to prevent blossom end rot
- Fertigate through drip: Water soluble fertilizers 19:19:19, 00:52:34, 13:00:45 at appropriate stages

## Staking
- **Stake plants at 30 cm height** using bamboo sticks (1.2–1.5 m long)
- Tie plants loosely with jute/soft rope at every 20–25 cm
- Staking prevents lodging, improves air circulation, reduces disease, and aids harvest

## Pest Management
| Pest | Damage Symptom | Control Measure |
|---|---|---|
| **Fruit Borer** (*Helicoverpa*) | Circular holes in fruits, larva inside | Emamectin benzoate 5SG @ 0.4g/L; Spinosad 45SC @ 0.3ml/L |
| **Whitefly** (*Bemisia tabaci*) | Sucks sap, transmits TYLCV | Yellow sticky traps; Imidacloprid 17.8SL @ 0.3ml/L |
| **Mites** (Red Spider Mite) | Bronzing/silvering of leaves, webbing | Abamectin 1.9EC @ 0.5ml/L; Fenazaquin 10EC @ 2ml/L |
| **Thrips** | Silvery streaks on leaves | Fipronil 5SC @ 1.5ml/L; Blue sticky traps |
| **Aphids** | Curling leaves, sticky honeydew | Dimethoate 30EC @ 2ml/L; Neem oil 3% |

## Disease Management
| Disease | Symptoms | Control |
|---|---|---|
| **Early Blight** (*Alternaria*) | Dark concentric rings on lower leaves | Mancozeb 75WP @ 2.5g/L; Azoxystrobin 23SC @ 1ml/L |
| **Late Blight** (*Phytophthora*) | Water-soaked greasy patches, white growth | Cymoxanil+Mancozeb @ 2g/L; Metalaxyl 8%+Mancozeb 64% WP |
| **TYLCV** (Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus) | Upward leaf curl, yellowing, stunting | Control whitefly vector; use Arka Rakshak/resistant varieties; Imidacloprid |
| **Bacterial Wilt** | Sudden wilting, vascular browning | Copper oxychloride 3g/L drench; use resistant varieties; soil solarization |
| **Fusarium Wilt** | One-sided yellowing, wilting | Trichoderma soil application; Carbendazim 2g/L drench |

## Spray Schedule (Recommended)
- **Week 1–2:** Carbendazim 1g/L (nursery/transplant drench)
- **Week 3–4:** Mancozeb 2.5g/L + Imidacloprid 0.3ml/L
- **Week 5–6:** Azoxystrobin 1ml/L + Emamectin 0.4g/L (fruiting stage)
- **Week 7–8:** Cymoxanil+Mancozeb 2g/L + Spinosad 0.3ml/L
- Always observe 7–14 day **Pre-Harvest Interval (PHI)** for all pesticides

## Harvest
- **Harvest at color break stage** – when fruit begins to turn from green to light red/pink
- This stage allows transport without damage; ripens in 3–5 days
- **Harvesting interval:** Every 2–3 days
- Handle fruits carefully to prevent bruising
- Expected yield: **30–40 tonnes/ha** (hybrid varieties under good management)

## Post-Harvest Tips
- Grade tomatoes by size and color for better market price
- Store at 10–13°C for 2–3 weeks shelf life
- Avoid stacking more than 3 layers in transport crates
